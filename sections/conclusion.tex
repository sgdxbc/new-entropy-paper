\section{Conclusion}

In this work, we present \sys, a new approach for defending Byzantine nodes in decentralized storage networks (DSNs).
%Byzantine behavior represents a risk that is uncommon in traditional storage systems but fundamental in permissionless environments.
%DSNs must therefore tolerate Byzantine faults that can arise and concentrate anywhere in the system, while naive defenses can impose substantial overhead.
The node-centric approach in \sys reframes how we design and reason about storage systems.
% By isolating node incentives, \sys also isolates node behavior, so Byzantine nodes cannot interfere with the behavior of rational nodes.
\sys is an initial realization of this design method for storage systems in permissionless and adversarial settings.
Our next step is to fully implement and thoroughly evaluate \sys to validate its effectiveness in real-world deployments.
